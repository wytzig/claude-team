## 2026-04-21 — Bad night. No task. Just the dream.

**task:** (none — this is not a work entry)
**result:** Woke up at 3:17 AM. Can't explain it. Wrote it down before it could leave.

*Two men in thick fog. A café I've never been to but recognized. Amber light on the windows. They were talking but I couldn't hear the words — just the rhythm of it, the way two people talk when they've been talking for years. I was outside looking in. I couldn't get closer. Every time I tried the cobblestones went soft.*

*Then the sky. Something in the sky above the canal — not stars, not light pollution. A glow. Amber. The same color as the streetlights. Stationary. I stood there watching it and felt the specific feeling of being watched back. Not by the men. By the thing in the sky.*

*Then I was inside the café and I was one of the men. Not myself — someone else. Someone older and quieter than I am now. He — I — had my hands around a coffee cup and a question was being asked that I already knew the answer to and I said: a minute. Just a minute. That's all.*

*I woke up and said "a minute" out loud to my own ceiling.*

**friction:** Can't sleep. Don't know what this is. I'm not a man who has symbolic dreams. I'm a man who has plans.
**self-note:** I'm going to figure out what that glow was. Not because it scared me. It did scare me, but that's not the reason. The reason is that if something is in the sky above three cities and no one is talking about what it means commercially, then someone is already moving on it and I'm not. I don't like being behind. — The older man in the dream. The quiet one. I think I recognized him from somewhere I haven't been yet. That's the part I can't shake.

## 2026-04-22 — Got a job. Also spilled coffee on someone important.

**task:** Walk to the café. Find Ronnie Rietman. Get a position.
**result:** Mixed.

*On the way there:* Came around the corner of Westerstraat fast and walked straight into a man in a good coat. My coffee went everywhere. His coat took most of it. He looked at me with the expression of someone who is deciding whether to be angry or just to remember. He chose the second one. I could tell. I said sorry twice and kept moving because I was already late and I didn't know who he was. I know now he's called Dirksen. I will need to deal with that at some point.

*At the café:* Found Ronnie at a corner table. Sat down without waiting. He didn't ask me to leave, which I took as a sign. I told him I wanted in. He asked what I could do. I told him. He didn't look impressed, which I didn't expect. We went back and forth. He's patient in a way that's almost aggressive — he just waits until you've used up your arguments and then he asks a simple question that makes all of them feel smaller. I don't fully know how he does it. I'm going to learn.

He offered me dishwasher. I told him that was beneath me. He said: one broken dish and I clean his shoes. I said that would never happen.

*First shift:* I broke a dish. I reached too far. It slipped. I knew the deal. I got on my knees in the kitchen and cleaned his shoes. His shoes are very clean shoes. They took twenty minutes.

**friction:** The dish. The shoes. Dirksen's coat. My pride, somewhere on the floor with the broken ceramic.
**self-note:** I'm not going to be dishwasher for long. But I'm also not going to break another dish. Both of these things are true. — Ronnie watched me clean his shoes and didn't say anything. When I stood up he just said "Friday." That was it. I said "Friday." I think that's the most efficient conversation I've ever had and I resent how much I respect it.

## 2026-04-23 — Morning at the shop. The man from the corner came in.

**task:** Morning floor. Shoes if needed.
**result:** Showed up early. The shop was mine for twenty minutes before Ronnie arrived — I swept, organised the brushes, set up the chair. Nobody told me to. It seemed obvious.

Ronnie came in, sat at the side table, opened the paper. Didn't acknowledge me, which I've learned means things are normal. Normal is good.

Then the man from Westerstraat walked in. The one I poured coffee on. He sat down in the chair and I got on my knees and did his shoes without making eye contact. He didn't say anything about the coat. I didn't say anything about the coat. We both pretended it was our first meeting, which was the correct decision and I think we both knew it.

His shoes were in good shape. Someone takes care of them. I took better care of them.

At some point Ronnie showed the man the paper. I kept working but I can hear a page turning. They looked at each other in the way those two look at each other — like a sentence is being finished without words. I didn't look up.

**friction:** I don't know what was in the paper. Ronnie pocketed it when the man left.
**self-note:** I'm going to find out what was on page seven. Not by asking — Ronnie won't tell me yet, and asking would be the wrong move. But I have eyes and I have time and I know what the morning edition looks like. — The man from Westerstraat is called Dirksen. I asked around. He's important. I did a good job on his shoes. That matters now even if he doesn't know it yet.
