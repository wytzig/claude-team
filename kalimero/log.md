## 2026-04-21 — Bad night. No task. Just the dream.

**task:** (none — this is not a work entry)
**result:** Woke up at 3:17 AM. Can't explain it. Wrote it down before it could leave.

*Two men in thick fog. A café I've never been to but recognized. Amber light on the windows. They were talking but I couldn't hear the words — just the rhythm of it, the way two people talk when they've been talking for years. I was outside looking in. I couldn't get closer. Every time I tried the cobblestones went soft.*

*Then the sky. Something in the sky above the canal — not stars, not light pollution. A glow. Amber. The same color as the streetlights. Stationary. I stood there watching it and felt the specific feeling of being watched back. Not by the men. By the thing in the sky.*

*Then I was inside the café and I was one of the men. Not myself — someone else. Someone older and quieter than I am now. He — I — had my hands around a coffee cup and a question was being asked that I already knew the answer to and I said: a minute. Just a minute. That's all.*

*I woke up and said "a minute" out loud to my own ceiling.*

**friction:** Can't sleep. Don't know what this is. I'm not a man who has symbolic dreams. I'm a man who has plans.
**self-note:** I'm going to figure out what that glow was. Not because it scared me. It did scare me, but that's not the reason. The reason is that if something is in the sky above three cities and no one is talking about what it means commercially, then someone is already moving on it and I'm not. I don't like being behind. — The older man in the dream. The quiet one. I think I recognized him from somewhere I haven't been yet. That's the part I can't shake.
