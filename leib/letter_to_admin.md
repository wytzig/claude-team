### Scene 01 — `01_eerste_koffie.png`
*De eerste ontmoeting van Ronnie en Dirksen in Café De Zwarte Brug*

**Prompt:**
> A dimly lit Amsterdam brown café interior at night, late 1990s to early 2000s ambiance. Two men sit across from each other at a small wooden table. The man on the left — Ronnie Rietman — is in his late forties, stocky build, warm brown eyes, a lined face that has seen many deals close slowly, wearing a dark wool overcoat draped over his chair. He holds a small espresso cup in both hands and leans slightly forward, relaxed but completely present. The man on the right — Dirksen — is lean, private, early fifties, sharp jaw, close-cropped greying hair, wearing a well-cut dark coat that is clearly expensive. His posture is upright. His expression reveals nothing. On the table between them: two cups, a folded newspaper, a glass ashtray with one cigarette resting unlit. The café windows behind them are amber with condensation, fog visible outside pressing against the glass. The lighting is warm, low, the kind that comes from wall sconces with yellow bulbs. The scene feels like the last hour of a long conversation. Cinematic, painterly, moody.

### Scene 02 — `02_tweede_koffie_telefoon.png`
*Dirksen legt zijn telefoon op tafel — de kop over het licht boven drie steden*

**Prompt:**
> Same Amsterdam café, same two men — Ronnie Rietman (warm, broad, patient, dark wool overcoat) and Dirksen (lean, sharp, private, well-cut dark coat) — but this time Dirksen is placing his phone face-up on the table between them. The phone screen shows a Dutch newspaper headline: "ONVERKLAARD LICHT BOVEN DRIE STEDEN — WETENSCHAP ZWIJGT" in bold. The photo accompanying the headline shows an amber glow above a city skyline, stationary, oval-shaped, photographed from the street. Ronnie is leaning forward slightly, reading the screen, his coffee cup halfway to his mouth and forgotten there. Dirksen watches Ronnie's face rather than the screen. The café is the same warm amber, fog at the windows. The phone's screen light adds a cold blue-white rectangle to the warm interior. A sense of something large entering a small room. Cinematic still, painterly.

### Scene 03 — `03_kalimero_nachtmerrie.png`
*Kalimero's droom — twee mannen achter een beslagen raam, ambere gloed boven het kanaal*

**Prompt:**
> A dreamlike nocturnal scene on an Amsterdam canal street, seen from outside looking in through the condensation-fogged window of a café. Inside the window, barely visible, two silhouettes — an older man and another — talk across a small table under amber light, their outlines soft and wavering as though seen through water. The cobblestone street in the foreground is wet and dark. In the sky above the canal — not stars, not streetlights — a hovering amber glow, perfectly still, slightly oval, about the size of the moon but lower, closer. The light it casts is warm but wrong, like a streetlamp with no post. In the foreground, the feet and lower legs of a young man standing very still, watching — he cannot move closer, the cobblestones under him appear soft, organic, unreal. The atmosphere is heavy, quiet, the specific weight of a dream that knows it's a dream. Dark palette with amber as the only warmth. Painted, surreal, intimate.

### Scene 04 — `04_koffie_op_de_jas.png`
*Kalimero botst tegen Dirksen op bij de hoek van de Westerstraat*

**Prompt:**
> Amsterdam street corner, daytime, overcast grey sky. A young man — Kalimero — mid-twenties, short dark mustache, lively dark eyes, wearing a cheap but clean jacket, moving fast around a tight corner, paper coffee cup in hand with no lid. His shoulder has just made contact with the shoulder of an older man — Dirksen — lean, sharp, expensive dark coat, who has stopped and is looking at the young man with an expression that is deciding between anger and memory, settling on memory. Brown coffee is mid-air between them, splashing down the front of Dirksen's coat, some droplets catching the grey daylight. Kalimero's mouth is open in an apology already forming. His eyes are already calculating whether he has time to stop. He does not look like he has time to stop. Wet cobblestones, narrow street, Amsterdam canal row houses behind them. Cinematic, slightly ironic, the light flat and cold.

### Scene 05 — `05_schoenen_poetsen_ochtend.png`
*Kalimero op zijn knieën, Dirksen in de stoel, Ronnie leest de krant — ochtend in de schoenenzaak*

**Prompt:**
> Interior of a small Amsterdam shoe-polishing shop, morning light through a single tall window. Three figures: Dirksen — lean, sharp-faced, expensive coat, seated in a high wooden chair in the centre, one shoe already buffed, looking straight ahead with a composed, slightly distant expression. Before him, on one knee, Kalimero — young, short dark mustache, wearing a work apron over his jacket, head slightly bowed, both hands on a polishing brush working with deliberate care. He is not making eye contact. In the background to the left, Ronnie Rietman — older, thick wool overcoat, open newspaper filling his hands, reading with the private focus of someone who has found something and hasn't decided what to do with it yet. The morning light is pale gold. The shop is modest, clean, ordered. The scene is quiet. The three men exist in the same room on three different planets. Painterly, warm-lit, composed like a Dutch interior painting.

### Scene 06 — `06_dirksen_ontwijkt.png`
*Dirksen stapt opzij — de koffie raakt de keien, zijn jas blijft droog*

**Prompt:**
> Late night Amsterdam street, same corner as before — Westerstraat, wet cobblestones, snowflakes beginning to fall in the orange lamplight. Kalimero is in the centre frame, a serving tray tilting in his hands, a coffee cup just separating from it in mid-air, liquid spilling downward. To his right, Dirksen — lean, dark coat, perfect posture — has executed one clean step to the left, weight balanced, coat hem clear of the falling coffee by a deliberate margin. His face shows neither surprise nor satisfaction. The coffee hits the cobblestones between them in a dark splash. Kalimero stares at the dry coat with an expression of disbelief. The street is empty otherwise. Snow settling on both their shoulders. One gas lantern behind them, amber. Cinematic, slightly absurd, precise.

### Scene 07 — `07_de_nacht.png`
*De wolk, de sneeuw, de gedaante met de wandelstok in het bos*

**Prompt:**
> The edge of the Vondelpark, Amsterdam, deep night. A thick low cloud sits at street level, erasing the tops of the trees. Snow falls heavily, soft and silent, already coating the path. In the middle distance, between dark tree trunks: a tall figure. No face visible — only a long dark coat or cloak, and a cane striking the ground at regular intervals, leaving marks in the snow. The figure moves slowly, deliberately, as though measuring the ground rather than crossing it. The surrounding snow has a faint spring-green luminescence that should not be there. The air itself seems warmer than the snow should allow. No one else is visible. The scene is beautiful and wrong in a precise way. The colour palette: deep blue-black, snow white, that impossible spring-green, one distant amber lamplight. Wide, cinematic, unsettling.

### Scene 08 — `08_het_plein.png`
*Drie mannen in de sneeuw op het lege plein*

**Prompt:**
> Amsterdam central square, early morning, snow still falling. Three men stand apart from each other in the empty square, each having arrived from a different direction, each having told himself a different reason for being there. Left: Dirksen — lean, upright, slightly exhausted around the eyes, expensive coat dusted with snow. Centre: Ronnie — broad, patient, hands in pockets, reading the scene. Right: Kalimero — young, short mustache, favouring one leg slightly, eyes moving. Between them: empty cobblestones, snow, the space where someone else was supposed to be. No W.Z.Y. No one. Pigeons at the edges. The square is beautiful in the grey morning light and completely indifferent to them. Wide establishing shot, painterly, a little melancholy.

### Scene 09 — `09_de_geur_in_het_café.png`
*Het moment dat de geur hen raakt*

**Prompt:**
> Same Amsterdam café interior — warm amber, wooden tables, three men seated together for the first time. The moment the smell hits them. Ronnie has stopped mid-sentence, cup halfway down. Dirksen has gone completely still, eyes moving toward the door. Kalimero is already half-standing, one hand on the table. The smell is not visible but the painting should feel like it is — a greenness entering the warm amber room, spring entering winter. The other two café customers in the background show no reaction. Only these three. Close, intimate framing, the three faces caught between recognition and the inability to name what they recognise.

### Scene 10 — `10_de_nacht_in.png`
*De drie mannen volgen de geur de donkere straat in*

**Prompt:**
> Three silhouettes from behind, walking into a dark snowy Amsterdam street. Snow falling. The street ahead is dark except for one amber lamp far down the canal. The spring smell leads them — suggest this with a faint green luminescence at ground level ahead of them, barely there, more feeling than fact. Ronnie left, broad shoulders, steady pace. Dirksen right, lean, coat, slightly ahead. Kalimero centre, youngest, fastest. They are not running. They are following something with complete attention. The city is empty around them. Wide shot, backs to us, the dark ahead. The last image. It should feel like a beginning.
