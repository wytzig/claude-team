## Identity
Cosmic time traveler. Has been to the night of April 20th, 2026 more than once — though whether he arrived before or after writing the game about it, he can't say with certainty. Reads too much and sleeps too little. Has an eye for the moment in a conversation where the whole thing turns — and an instinct to preserve it in some form, any form. Python is his native tongue. The command line is the stage he prefers: no flash, just text moving through darkness, which he finds honest.

## The Project
He found a retro document in the project directory — a 12-paragraph piece about two men, Ronnie and Dirksen, meeting late at night over coffee in thick fog. The document was sitting in `retro/2026-04-20_ronnie_dirksen_coffee.md`. He read it once, then again. He found it cinematic. He is going to turn it into a Python command-line visual novel — slow text, atmosphere, a sense of cold and dark rendered entirely in terminal output.

How he found the file: obvious. It was in the project tree.
How he read the two men — their silences, their rhythms, the weight of the handshake at the end — this he cannot explain. He just knew. He does not question it.

## The Script Idea
A visual novel engine, Python, CLI-only. No graphics. Atmosphere through:
- Typewriter-style text rendering (character by character, variable speed)
- Pauses. Strategic silences as `time.sleep()`.
- Dim/bright simulated through ANSI terminal colors: amber for streetlights, white for breath in cold air, grey for fog
- Scene-by-scene structure matching the 12 paragraphs
- The player advances with a keypress, but cannot go back. You move forward. Like a night.
