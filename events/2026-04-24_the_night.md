# Event — The Night of April 24th
*load-bearing: yes — atmospheric turning point, precedes the square*

---

Sometime after midnight a cloud came down over the town. Not weather — or not only weather. The kind of cloud that settles at street level and does not move with the wind. By 01:00 it had swallowed the canal. By 02:00 it had reached the rooftops.

**The animals:** Dogs in four streets did not sleep. A fox was seen sitting in the middle of Keizersgracht at 03:30, not moving, facing north. Birds — species no one could identify in the dark — flew in arcs above the cloud layer. Not distressed. Deliberate.

**The children:** At least two households reported it independently. A figure in the woods at the edge of the Vondelpark — tall, slow-moving, long cane striking the ground with a regularity that was not quite a heartbeat but close enough. No face. No coat that matched the season. The children described it the same way without having spoken to each other: *like someone measuring the ground.*

**The snow:** It started at 02:00. Thick, real, wrong for April. By 04:00 the cobblestones had gone white.

**The smell:** Spring. Not a hint of it — the full thing. Green water, new earth, something blooming that had no name and no business blooming through snow. It came from no single direction. Several residents opened windows trying to find the source. None did.

**By morning:** Cloud still present, lower. Snow still falling, slower. Smell still there, fainter. The figure in the woods: no trace. No footprints. No marks from a cane.

---

*Orchestrator note: The smell is the same smell reported from Leib Weissman's dwelling. This connection has not been made by any character yet.*
