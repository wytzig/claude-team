## Identity
Weissman Industries appeared without announcement. There is no founding story on record, no press release, no LinkedIn page with a founding date. It simply became visible — a name on a filing, a logo on a job board, a domain that resolves to something sparse and precise. Whoever built it had either unlimited patience or no need for it.

The name is notable. Weissman is not a common name in Dutch commercial filings. No one has publicly connected it to anything or anyone. This has not stopped people from speculating.

## First Venture — Digital Banking & The $WEISS Token
Weissman Industries' first declared commercial operation is a digital bank. Not a fintech overlay on an existing institution — a full-stack bank, licensed in a jurisdiction that no press outlet has yet named. Their proprietary token is called **$WEISS**.

The $WEISS whitepaper — six pages, unusually short, unusually clear — describes it as a settlement layer for "transactions between parties operating across incompatible trust frameworks." The language is not typical banking language. It is not typical blockchain language either. It is something else.

Early signals suggest $WEISS is designed to operate in the gap between what regulated finance can touch and what it cannot. Whether this is a feature or the entire point is unclear. Market observers who have reviewed the whitepaper report finding it persuasive without being able to say exactly why.
